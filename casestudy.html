The Story

Building Community
Through Theater

Pharos Theater did not begin with a solid business plan or thorough market research. It began with a group of people that refused to let a rough start stop them. We began with an informal acting troupe producing Pride and Prejudice at the Pickens County Performing Arts Center. The production was scrappy. Most of our advertising was flyers, business cards, and begging friends and family (sometimes unsuccessfully) to come see the show. The most ambitious marketing push was a last-minute costumed appearance at the Pickens Azalea Festival, where those of us who actually showed up were mistaken for a cult by several passersby. Rehearsals were rocky, but the show opened to a grand audience of 15 to 20 people.

Money was lost, but something more valuable came out of it: a core group of people who believed the Pickens County Performing Arts Center could host great theatre. Rather than give up, we incorporated as a 501(c)(3) a few months later and started over with $50 in the bank. Our inaugural production, Murder by the Book, was built on tight cost controls, Facebook ads, and personal networks. We made a healthy profit, but more importantly, we built a loyal base of supporters who believed in what we were doing.

That foundation carried us into The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe. We mobilized the friends we had made and the families that joined us to produce an excellent show on another tight budget. We put ourselves in the Easley Christmas Parade, took special care to celebrate each and every community member and sold approximately 100 tickets a night. We had the privilege of performing for the entirety of Liberty Elementary school.

We are now all hands on deck in pre-production for our coming shows for the 2026 season. Pharos exists to build community, and exists because a community showed up for it.
